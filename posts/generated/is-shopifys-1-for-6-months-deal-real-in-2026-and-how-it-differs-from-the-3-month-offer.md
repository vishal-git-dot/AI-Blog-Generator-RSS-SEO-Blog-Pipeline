# Is Shopify's $1 for 6 Months Deal Real in 2026? (And How It Differs From the 3-Month Offer)

## Overview

If you build or maintain Shopify stores for clients, you have fielded this question: "I saw a deal — $1 a month for 6 months. Is that real, or is it one of those too-good-to-be-true things?" It is a fair thing to ask before committing a business to a platform, and the honest answer is more nuanced than the affiliate blogs ranking for that query make it sound — half of them are quoting numbers Shopify retired a year ago. This is the developer-facing short version: what a client is actually offered at signup in 2026, where the real six-month price comes from, and one new promo almost nobody has written up correctly yet. Every figure below was checked against Shopify's own live pages on July 21, 2026 — the terms genuinely move, so treat any undated number you read elsewhere with suspicion. The interactive version — a post-trial cost calculator, the full eligibility matrix, and a monthly-updated offer tracker — lives on the canonical post . What follows is the text summary you can forward to a client to answer "is it real" without them needing to click through. The short answer Yes, a $1/month-for-6-months Shopify deal is real — but "real" needs three footnotes, and only one of them is the deal most people picture. Here is the whole map. What your client actually sees at signup (verified July 21, 2026) The public offer on shopify.com/pricing right now reads: "Try 3 days free, then $1/month for 3 months." That is three days of free trial, then a $1/month promotional rate for three months — not six. The amount renders as $1 / €1 / £1 per month depending on the store's region ; it is the same offer in local currency. So the default a US, UK, or EU client is shown today is the three-month version. If you want the deeper mechanics of that standard offer, I break it down in the $1 for 3 months guide . The part that matters for scoping a client engagement: the promotional term is assigned by Shopify per visitor and locked at signup — it cannot be extended or converted afterward. Where the real six-month price comes from There is exactly one confirmed route to $1/month for six months, and it is a program , not a public banner: Shopify's 1MBB (1 Million Black Businesses) partnership. It offers the Basic plan at $1/month for the first six months, limited to eligible merchants in the US and Canada . Two things to flag to a client before they get excited: It is a paid discount, not a free trial. They pay $1/month (six real payments), they are not getting six free months. Eligibility is real and enforced. It is built for a specific group of entrepreneurs; it is not a loophole anyone can trigger with a referral link. If you have seen a guide describing 1MBB as a "120-day free trial," it is out of date — that framing contradicts Shopify's live 1MBB page as of July 21, 2026. Quoting it to a client is how you lose credibility three weeks into a build. There is also a separate "6 months on us" offer for Shopify Plus — but that is an enterprise migration incentive tied to a three-year commitment , aimed at brands moving off another platform. It is not the standard-plan trial anyone means when they say "the $1 deal." The part almost nobody has covered correctly: sales credits Here is the genuinely new thing, and the one where I have watched aggregator blogs invent numbers. As of July 21, 2026, shopify.com/free-trial advertises, on top of the $1/month pricing: "earn up to $8,000 in credits as you sell." These are subscription credits, not cash — you accrue 1% back on sales made through Shopify Payments (0.5% on other gateways) over your first six months. The "up to $8,000" is the ceiling for the Advanced plan. Each plan has its own cap, per shopify.com/pricing : Plan Credit cap (USD) Basic $4,500 Grow $6,500 Advanced $8,000 Plus $13,000 If you see $5,000 / $7,500 / $10,000 floating around in other write-ups, those are fabricated — the real caps are the four above, and the currency follows the store's region (the same $1 / €1 / £1 logic applies). Treat the credit as a bonus on top of the runway, not a reason to steer a client into overspending to chase it. Six months vs three months: what actually differs For a client comparing the two, the only real difference is duration . Features, app access, and card-processing fees are identical on both. In subscription terms: 3-month deal: about $3 total in platform fees. 6-month deal: about $6 total in platform fees. That is it — three extra dollars buys twice the low-cost runway. The difference that matters is not the three dollars; it is the extra optimization window . With three months, most stores are still in setup when the promo ends. Six months gets a client through a real learn-and-iterate cycle before standard pricing applies. After the promo, the plan reverts to standard pricing (Basic is $39/month, or $29/month on annual billing, per shopify.com/pricing ), and Shopify does not extend or reset promotional pricing — so put that renewal date on the project calendar. What to tell a client (the 60-second version) The public deal in 2026 is 3 days free, then $1/month for 3 months — assume that, not six. $1/month for six months is real but program-gated (1MBB; US/Canada; Basic; a paid discount). Do not delay a launch waiting for a six-month term to appear — it is assigned per visitor and cannot be summoned. There is a real up-to-$8,000-in-sales-credits promo layered on top; verify the exact per-plan caps, ignore the invented ones. Read the exact term shown at signup — it is locked the moment the account is created.
