Understanding the Chip War: Key Definitions and Context The ongoing chip war refers to the geopolitical tension surrounding semiconductor technology and production. This conflict has significant implications for global supply chains, particularly as the U.S. imposes restrictions on exports of advanced technologies to China. As noted by ASML CEO Christophe Fouquet, China is currently limited to older-generation deep ultraviolet tools , which are essential for semiconductor manufacturing. The MATCH Act aims to restrict access to these tools, potentially stifling innovation in chip development. [INTERNAL:chip-manufacturing|Understanding Semiconductor Production] Key Definitions Deep Ultraviolet Tools : Specialized equipment used in photolithography to etch circuits onto silicon wafers. MATCH Act : Legislation aimed at restricting the export of advanced semiconductor technologies to specific countries, notably China. Semiconductor Supply Chain : The network of organizations involved in the design, manufacturing, and distribution of semiconductor devices. Mechanisms and Architecture: How Chip Manufacturing Works Chip manufacturing involves several complex processes, primarily photolithography, etching, and doping. The use of deep ultraviolet (DUV) lithography is critical in creating intricate circuit patterns on silicon wafers. Photolithography Process Wafer Preparation : Silicon wafers are cleaned and coated with a light-sensitive material called photoresist. Exposure : The wafer is exposed to ultraviolet light through a mask that defines the circuit pattern. Development : The exposed photoresist is developed, revealing the pattern on the wafer. Etching : Unprotected areas of silicon are etched away to form the circuit. Doping : Impurities are introduced to modify electrical properties. Challenges in Chip Manufacturing Equipment Costs : Advanced DUV tools are expensive, limiting access for many manufacturers. Technological Expertise : Requires skilled personnel to operate complex machinery and handle intricate processes.
