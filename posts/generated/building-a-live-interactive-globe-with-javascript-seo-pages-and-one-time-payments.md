# Building a live interactive globe with JavaScript, SEO pages, and one-time payments

## Overview

I’ve been building a web project called The Signal. The idea is simple: A person opens a live globe, chooses a city, claims a light, and leaves one public sentence. That sentence can be a thought, a memory, a dedication, a message to someone, or just a small mark that they were here. I wanted to build something that feels different from a normal social feed. No endless scrolling as the main experience. No chasing likes as the core mechanic. More like a shared map of small human traces. Why a globe? A globe makes the idea feel physical. A sentence is not just floating in a feed. It belongs somewhere. It has a city attached to it. It becomes a point of light. That tiny detail changes the feeling of the product. It makes the message feel less like content and more like presence. The product The Signal lets people: claim a light from their city attach one public sentence view other lights on the globe open individual light pages create memorial lights trigger small visual actions on the map The business model is based on one-time digital purchases, not subscriptions. That felt important. A permanent light should not feel like a monthly bill. What I’m trying to figure out The hard part is not the globe. The hard part is trust. Why would a stranger leave a sentence? Why would they claim a light? What makes the map feel alive instead of empty? How much explanation is too much? Those are the questions I’m testing now. What I’d love feedback on If you open it, I’d love to know: Do you understand the idea in the first few seconds? Does the globe make you want to interact with it? Does “one sentence from your city” feel clear? What would make you trust it more? Here’s the project: https://thesignal.place
