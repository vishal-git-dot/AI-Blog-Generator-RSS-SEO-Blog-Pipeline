# RAG vs Long-Context Models for Enterprise AI

## Overview

Long-context models can read huge inputs in one pass, which raises the question: do you still need RAG? For accuracy and scale, yes. Loading everything into the window brings two problems: cost and latency rise as the window fills the model struggles to pinpoint one fact in a massive block RAG finds the exact passages a question needs, grounds the answer in them, and cites the source. That stays accurate and verifiable across knowledge bases far larger than any window could hold, without reprocessing everything per query. CustomGPT.ai uses RAG so enterprises get reliable, citable answers without the long-context tax in cost and latency. The right question is not how much a model can read. It is how accurately it can answer, and how easily a human can verify it. On both, retrieval wins for enterprise work.
