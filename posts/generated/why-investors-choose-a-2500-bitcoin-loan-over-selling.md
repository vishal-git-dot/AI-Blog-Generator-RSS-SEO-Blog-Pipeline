The Tax Advantage of Borrowing vs. Selling When you sell Bitcoin for more than you paid, the IRS views that as a taxable event — you've "realized" a capital gain . The rates aren't friendly: Short-term gains (under one year) are taxed as ordinary income up to 37% Long-term gains face 0%, 15%, or 20% depending on income, plus potential state taxes and the 3.8% Net Investment Income Tax for high earners Crypto-backed loans, however, are not taxable events. When you take out a loan to borrow against Bitcoin, you're receiving loan proceeds, not selling an asset. The IRS doesn't consider borrowed money as income because you have an obligation to repay it . A Simple Example Imagine you bought 1 Bitcoin at $30,000. It's now worth $68,000 and you need $27,000 .
