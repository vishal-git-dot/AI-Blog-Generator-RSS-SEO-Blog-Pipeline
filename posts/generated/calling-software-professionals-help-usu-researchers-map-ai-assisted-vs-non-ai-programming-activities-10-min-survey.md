# Calling Software Professionals: Help USU Researchers Map AI-Assisted vs. Non-AI Programming Activities (10-Min Survey)

## Overview

How Do Software Professionals Distinguish AI-Assisted Programming from Programming Without AI Assistance? AI-assisted programming is becoming increasingly common, but not every programming activity fits neatly into a single category. Developers may plan solutions, write code, debug problems, review suggestions, test implementations, or revise existing programs. Some of these activities may be performed with AI assistance, while others may be performed without it. This raises an important research question: How representative are different programming activities of programming performed with AI assistance compared with programming performed without AI assistance? Researchers at Utah State University's School of Computing are conducting a study to better understand how software professionals evaluate this distinction. Why software professionals' perspectives matter Programming practices differ across professional roles, technologies, projects, and workplaces. A programming activity that one developer strongly associates with AI-assisted work may be viewed differently by another developer. Gathering ratings from software professionals can help researchers develop a more carefully calibrated understanding of how programming activities are perceived across AI-assisted and non-AI-assisted settings. What does participation involve? Participants will complete a short online calibration survey. During the survey, participants will rate programming activities according to how representative they are of: Programming performed with AI assistance Programming performed without AI assistance The survey takes approximately 10 minutes . Who is invited? We are inviting software professionals who are at least 18 years of age and are residents of the United States to participate.
