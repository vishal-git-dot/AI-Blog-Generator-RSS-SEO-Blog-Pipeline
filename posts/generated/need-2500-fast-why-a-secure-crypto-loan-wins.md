For example, if your lender requires a 50% LTV and you need $2,500, you must deposit $5,000 worth of crypto. Because crypto prices are volatile, your LTV ratio can shift. If the price of your collateral falls, your LTV rises, moving your loan closer to a "margin call." To ensure your loan remains secure, keep these best practices in mind: Borrow Conservatively: Aim for a lower LTV—like 30% or 40%—to provide a safety buffer against market dips. Monitor Your Dashboard: Check your account status regularly to stay ahead of any market volatility. Keep a Buffer: Maintain a small amount of extra capital or stablecoins to "top up" your collateral if needed, preventing forced liquidations.
