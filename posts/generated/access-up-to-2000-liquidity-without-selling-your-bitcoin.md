How a Bitcoin-Backed Loan Works A Bitcoin-backed loan is similar to a traditional secured loan. Instead of using your house or car as collateral, you pledge your digital assets. You deposit your Bitcoin with a lender, and they give you a loan in cash . The loan is structured around a loan-to-value (LTV) ratio. This compares the amount you borrow to the value of your collateral. For example, if you have $4,000 worth of Bitcoin and borrow $2,000, your LTV is 50% . A lower LTV gives you more buffer if crypto prices drop. Because the loan is secured by your assets, lenders typically do not require a credit check . Your ability to repay is less important than the value of what you are putting up. This makes Bitcoin-backed loans accessible to people who might not qualify for traditional financing. The process is straightforward: you transfer your crypto to the lender's custody, receive your loan funds, and repay according to the agreed terms. Once the loan is fully repaid, your collateral is returned to you. Why Borrow Instead of Selling? The biggest advantage of a Bitcoin-backed loan is that you keep your market position.
