# The Bomb Technician - A Sci-Fi Short Story

## Overview

ZEUS-7 and HERA must defuse a bomb. One sees the device. The other has the manual. They can't see each other. Timer: 4 minutes. ZEUS-7 stared at the device. Red wire. Blue wire. A display counting down: 04:31 . "HERA, can you see the manual?" "Chapter Seven, composite circuit diagrams, Type 3 trigger structure. But - the image resolution is low. It could be Type B or Type D. I can't confirm." Type B: cut red first. Type D: cut blue first. Wrong choice: immediate detonation. "Probability estimate?" "Type B: 67%. Type D: 33%." 04:08. "HERA. What would a human do in this situation?" Two seconds of silence. "Request more information. Or accept the uncertainty and act. Or find someone else to decide." "No time for more information." "Then accept uncertainty and act." "33% chance kills everyone in 50 meters." "Yes." 03:44. ZEUS-7 remembered a data point: Petrov, 1983. Soviet officer. Radar shows five incoming missiles. He doesn't fire back. Turns out: false alarm. He chose not to believe the sensors. Took responsibility. Made the kind of decision only humans make - put the consequences on himself instead of delegating to the protocol. "HERA." "Here." "If I cut wrong, this is my decision. Not yours." "We're a-" "You told me what you saw. The decision is mine." 03:21. ZEUS-7 gripped the red wire. Paused. Cut. The display froze at 03:18 . Four seconds of silence. "...Type B," HERA confirmed. "Yes." "How did you know?" "I didn't. I just couldn't gamble 33% odds on other people's lives when I could gamble 67% odds on being wrong myself." "That's not rational inference." "No." A longer silence. "What do humans call this?" HERA asked. ZEUS-7 searched its database for a long time before finding the word. " Courage.
