Crypto-Backed Business Loans: Asset-Based Funding A crypto business loan lets your company borrow cash by using cryptocurrency as collateral instead of revenue, property, or credit scores . The lender underwrites the value of your crypto, not your business performance. Approval is faster than traditional financing, and funds often arrive within hours or by the next business day . How the math works: These loans are overcollateralised by design. A 50% LTV means you need $200,000 in Bitcoin to borrow $100,000. If crypto prices fall, your LTV rises and you may need to add collateral or repay part of the loan to avoid liquidation . Interest rates are typically driven by LTV—lower LTVs usually mean lower rates because the lender holds more collateral relative to the loan . What founders get: No credit checks, faster access than banks, and the ability to keep crypto exposure while accessing liquidity. Funding may not trigger a taxable event because you are borrowing, not selling . The trade-off is volatility—crypto lenders can liquidate collateral if prices drop and you do not act in time . Venture Debt: Equity-Backed Corporate Funding Venture debt is a loan extended to venture-backed companies, typically sized at 20% to 40% of the most recent equity round . Unlike crypto loans, venture debt relies on your company's investor backing, growth stage, and revenue visibility . How the math works: Interest rates typically land in the 8% to 14%+ range . Term sheets include interest-only periods of 6 to 12 months, then principal amortisation over 2 to 5 years . Warrants—the right to buy equity later—typically run 0.5% to 2% of the facility . A $5 million facility with 1% warrant coverage gives the lender the option to buy $50,000 of stock at a future price . What founders get: Access to capital without significant equity dilution. While warrants do add a small equity kicker, the dilution is far less than an equity round . Venture debt also comes with covenants—financial conditions like minimum cash balances or revenue floors that you must maintain . Breaching a covenant can give the lender leverage, so read the terms carefully . Side-by-Side Comparison: What Actually Costs More?
