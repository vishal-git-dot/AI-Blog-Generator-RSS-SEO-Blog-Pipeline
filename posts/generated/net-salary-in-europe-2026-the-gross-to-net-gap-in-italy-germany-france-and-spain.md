# Net Salary in Europe 2026: The Gross-to-Net Gap in Italy, Germany, France and Spain

## Overview

Most European workers are surprised when they see their first payslip. The number on the contract and the number on the payslip rarely match — and the gap is larger than most people expect. In 2026, the gross-to-net gap varies dramatically across the four largest economies in Europe. Here's what workers actually take home. Italy: IRPEF + INPS Italy applies a progressive IRPEF (income tax) on top of INPS social contributions (~9.19% employee side). A €30,000 gross salary yields roughly €21,500 net — a 28% deduction.
