Frequently Asked Questions What is the technical function of tokenization in digital payment platform architecture? Tokenization is a security process that replaces sensitive primary account numbers (PANs)—such as credit card numbers or bank routing codes—with a unique surrogate value known as a token. In payment gateway architecture, this token has no exploitable external meaning or value if intercepted by malicious actors during transmission. When a transaction occurs, the payment platform securely translates the token back within a PCI-compliant vault environment to authorize the transfer. This decoupling of sensitive data from merchant databases drastically reduces the impact of data breaches in e-commerce ecosystems. How do micro-deposits verify ownership of linked bank accounts during onboarding? Micro-deposits serve as a verification method where a financial platform initiates two small, random monetary transfers (typically under one dollar) to a user's external bank account. Because accessing the exact deposit amounts requires logging into the external bank statement, this method verifies that the user has legitimate control over the linked financial instrument. Once the user enters the correct deposit amounts back into the payment platform, the banking connection is verified, authorizing future funds transfers between the accounts.
