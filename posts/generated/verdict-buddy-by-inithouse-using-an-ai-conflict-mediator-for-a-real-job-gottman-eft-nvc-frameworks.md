# Verdict Buddy by Inithouse: using an AI conflict mediator for a real job (Gottman + EFT + NVC frameworks)

## Overview

We had a roommate conflict at our office. Two people sharing a workspace, both reasonable, both annoyed. One wanted music playing during the day; the other needed silence to concentrate. The usual compromise attempts had failed: headphones only worked for one side, alternating days pleased nobody, and the conversation kept circling without landing anywhere. So we ran it through Verdict Buddy, the AI conflict mediator we built at Inithouse. Not because the conflict was dramatic, but because it was stuck. What Verdict Buddy actually does Verdict Buddy takes a conflict description and applies four established psychological frameworks to it: Gottman's Four Horsemen, Emotionally Focused Therapy (EFT), Nonviolent Communication (NVC), and the Harvard Negotiation framework. It scores the tension, maps each person's perspective, identifies the conflict pattern, and returns a structured verdict with three prioritized actions. The whole thing runs in the browser. No account. No signup. The verdict comes back in about two minutes. The workspace music conflict, step by step I opened verdictbuddy.com and picked the Solo mode. (There are also Couple and Group modes where multiple parties can submit their sides separately, but for a quick test, Solo works.) I described the situation in plain language: two colleagues sharing a room, one wants background music, one needs quiet, compromise attempts have failed, both feel the other is being unreasonable. Verdict Buddy classified the conflict pattern within seconds. The Gottman analysis flagged stonewalling on both sides. The NVC lens pointed out that neither person had stated their actual need directly. The person wanting music wasn't asking for entertainment; they needed something to mask intrusive ambient noise. The person wanting silence wasn't demanding total quiet; they needed uninterrupted focus blocks. The verdict came back structured: a tension score, a summary of each perspective translated into framework terms, and three concrete next steps. The top recommendation was to reframe the negotiation around the underlying needs (noise masking vs. focus blocks) rather than the surface positions (music vs. silence). Did the recommendation hold up? I showed the verdict to both people involved. The reaction was interesting. Neither felt attacked. The framework language gave them a way to talk about the conflict without it feeling personal. "Stonewalling" sounds clinical, but it also sounds precise. Both recognized the pattern. They ended up testing a solution the verdict hadn't explicitly suggested but that followed from the reframed needs: a white noise machine during shared hours and designated quiet blocks. Three weeks later, the arrangement is still holding. What 290 ratings taught us about conflict categories Verdict Buddy currently holds a 4.9 out of 5 rating from 290+ users. When we look at the conflict types people bring in, relationship disagreements account for the largest share, but workplace and roommate conflicts come in close behind. Family conflicts (holiday logistics, parenting disagreements) spike seasonally. The pattern that keeps showing up: most conflicts that feel intractable are actually positional standoffs where both sides have compatible underlying needs. The frameworks catch this reliably. Gottman spots the destructive communication pattern, NVC identifies the unspoken needs, and the Harvard model suggests the reframe. Where Verdict Buddy fits This is not therapy. We say this clearly on the site and we mean it. A trained therapist brings context, history, and clinical judgment that no tool replaces. What Verdict Buddy does is give you a structured read on a specific disagreement, grounded in frameworks that therapists actually use, delivered in two minutes with no waiting room. For the stuck-in-a-loop conflicts (the ones where you keep having the same argument without making progress), that structured read can be the thing that breaks the cycle. Verdict Buddy is one of the products in the Inithouse portfolio. We build AI tools that do specific jobs well. Ziva Fotka turns static photos into short living videos (10,000+ photos animated).
