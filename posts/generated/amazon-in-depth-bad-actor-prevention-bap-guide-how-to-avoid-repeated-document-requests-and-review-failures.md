# Amazon In-Depth Bad Actor Prevention (BAP) Guide: How to Avoid Repeated Document Requests and Review Failures

## Overview

As Amazon tightly seals its compliance loops, Bad Actor Prevention has become a standard procedure. The platform verifies not only the completeness of documents but primarily assesses account authenticity and entity consistency. In recent years, Bad Actor Prevention has evolved into a multi-dimensional risk model that combines cross-verification of data with account environment analysis. Even if documents are authentic, discrepancies can still trigger requests for additional materials or video verification. This article breaks down the trigger causes, document preparation, and review workflows to guide you through the process. I. Why Do Accounts Frequently Trigger Amazon BAP? For the platform, the purpose of BAP is not to seek out violating sellers, but to verify "whether this entity is authentic, whether the information is trustworthy, and whether the business possesses continuous operational capacity" before full business activities begin. The platform establishes risk assessments across 4 key dimensions: Entity Authenticity: Verifying whether the business license, legal representative, registered address, and receiving bank account align perfectly to form a complete entity loop. Document Consistency: Reviewing whether discrepancies in names, addresses, or dates exist between the backend information and submitted physical materials. Operational Environment Trustworthiness: Evaluating whether login IPs, devices, and network environments remain stable over the long term, looking out for frequent switching or anomalous login footprints. Historical Risk Logs: Scanning for risk signals such as associated accounts, past review histories, anomalous logins, or policy violations. II. How to Improve Your Pass Rate for Amazon BAP? 1、Build a Stable and Independent Account Environment The login environment serves as critical criteria for the platform to evaluate entity authenticity. If you frequently change your IP, log in from different regions, or share the same network across multiple accounts during the review window, your risk score will increase, disrupting review progress. Key areas to focus on: Reduce High-Risk Modifications: Avoid modifying core entity data, altering receiving bank accounts, or adjusting admin permissions unless absolutely necessary, to prevent triggering a platform re-evaluation. Maintain a Fixed Login Setup: Use the same hardware, browser, and network to access the backend. Avoid changing devices frequently or sharing accounts among multiple people to help the platform establish a continuous, stable account profile. Utilize a Stable Network Environment: Avoid using shared proxies or high-risk networks. For teams managing multiple stores over the long term, professional proxy services with dedicated static residential proxy capabilities (such as IPFoxy) can provide fixed local IPs to give each store an independent network environment, reducing anomaly detection flags caused by IP fluctuations. 2、Diagnose Your BAP Type Different review notifications focus on different priorities. Submitting documents blindly without identifying the specific review type can lead to mismatched materials and repeated document requests. 3、Establish Complete Logical Consistency Across Documents During the actual review, the platform does not merely check if a single document is authentic; it looks for a complete logical loop among all submitted documents. If historical data changes have occurred, explain the reasons proactively in a Cover Letter instead of waiting for reviewers to raise red flags. Follow these principles when organizing your documents: Unify Entity Information: Ensure company names, legal representative names, and registered addresses match backend data perfectly, avoiding abbreviations, translation variances, or formatting inconsistencies. Keep Materials Up-to-Date: Use utility bills, bank statements, and related documents issued within the last three months to prevent re-submission requests due to expired documentation. Map Clear Document Relationships: Every submitted file should correspond directly to a specific piece of information in the backend, forming an enclosed data loop rather than existing as isolated files. 4、Prepare for Video Verification in Advance Video verification typically revolves around corporate entities, legal representative identities, and actual business operations. Familiarize yourself with all submitted materials beforehand and ensure your answers align with the documents. Steps to prepare for video verification: Gather All Original Documents: Have original copies of the business license, ID card, address proofs, and bank data ready for on-camera inspection at the reviewer's request. Ensure Stable Hardware and Network: Avoid network disconnections or device switching during the video call to maintain a smooth review experience. Deliver Authentic and Consistent Answers: If faced with an unfamiliar question, state the facts honestly rather than guessing or modifying answers on the fly. 5、Submit a Complete Chain of Evidence at Once A logical chain of evidence helps reviewers understand your account status quickly, improving review efficiency. Organize all materials in a structured sequence and pair them with a concise Cover Letter explaining the documentation. Key components to include: Basic Account Information: Store name, Merchant Token, and registered email to help reviewers locate your account quickly. Clarification of Review Issues: Briefly outline the purpose of the submission and address each review requirement listed in the platform notification item by item. Attachment Inventory: Number all submitted files and clarify what each document verifies, making it easy for the reviewer to audit. III. Post-Submission: BAP Workflows and Outcome Strategies 1、The BAP Workflow The initial automated screening primarily checks if documents are complete and formatted correctly. Once the case moves to manual review, the focus shifts to document consistency, cross-referenced with account history and cumulative risk scores. This manual stage is highly time-consuming and is where sellers most frequently receive supplementary data requests or video verification notices. Review timelines generally follow these patterns: Complete Documents & Low Risk: Results are typically delivered within a few business days to a week. Additional Materials Required: The review cycle resets. Submit the requested materials as quickly as possible and avoid uploading different versions of the same file repeatedly. Routed to Video Verification: Sellers must wait for a platform scheduling notice and will enter final evaluation after completing the verification call. 2、Action Strategies for Different BAP Outcomes IV. FAQ Q1: If BAP takes longer, does it mean my account is in greater danger? Not necessarily. A prolonged timeline usually means the system cannot make a rapid automated determination and requires manual intervention. This happens when there are slight data inconsistencies or unstable behavioral logs, which makes the review more complex but does not equate to policy violations or a high-risk account. Q2: Will opening a new store under the same company trigger BAP again? Most likely. The system evaluates more than just your business documentation; it monitors login footprints and initialization behaviors. If the new store's startup environment or operational patterns deviate significantly, it can trigger the BAP workflow again despite using identical entity data. Q3: Why is the wait time even longer after submitting additional materials? Submitting supplementary documents triggers a secondary comparison process. The system re-verifies consistency between old and new materials. If doubts persist, manual review times extend further. This is a complete re-verification process, not a simple accelerated filing. Q4: Is the account permanently safe after passing BAP? No. Clearing the review means your account cleared the current assessment. If environment variables change or core information is adjusted in the future, the continuous risk control mechanism can trigger automated compliance reviews again. V. Conclusion Amazon BAP functions fundamentally as a continuous risk evaluation mechanism rather than a one-time document audit. The platform cross-references information consistency, behavioral stability, and historical logs to judge whether an account possesses trustworthy operational capacity. Consequently, clearing the review depends on whether your overall account model is self-consistent, not just whether you have gathered all necessary materials. When your entity information, operational environment, and behavioral paths align seamlessly, the review progresses smoothly; conversely, even if your documents are real, logical gaps can trap the account in repeated verification loops.
