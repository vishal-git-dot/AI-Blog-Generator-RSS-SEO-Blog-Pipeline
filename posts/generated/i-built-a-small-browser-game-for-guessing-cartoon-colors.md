# I built a small browser game for guessing cartoon colors

## Overview

I recently built a small browser game called ToonTone. The idea started from a simple question: how well do I actually remember the colors of cartoon and anime characters? Most guessing games ask you to recognize a name, an image, or a sound. I wanted to make something a little different: show a character prompt, then let the player adjust hue, saturation, and brightness until the color feels close enough. The first version was intentionally simple. A normal round works like this: The game gives you a cartoon or anime character You try to match that character's main color You adjust hue, saturation, and brightness The game scores how close your color is After five rounds, you get a final score image you can share I wanted the whole thing to feel quick. No account, no setup, no long onboarding. Just open the browser and play one round. One surprisingly tricky part was the scoring. Color is not always as obvious as it sounds. Some characters have multiple iconic colors, and different people remember them differently. I had to keep the scoring forgiving enough to feel fair, but strict enough that a good score still feels earned. I also added a daily challenge and a leaderboard later, because the game felt more fun when the same set of prompts could be compared with other players. Here is one of my recent results: I got 6.1/10 this time. Jake the Dog was almost perfect for me, but Mr. Bean and a couple of other rounds pulled the score down. That is part of what makes the game fun: sometimes the color you remember is not as accurate as you thought. The project is still small, but it was a fun 0-to-1 build because the core loop was clear from the beginning: guess a color, compare the result, and try again. You can try it here: https://toontonegame.app/ If you play a round, I would be curious whether the scoring feels fair or too strict.
