# 06: Best Laptop Keyboard Layouts Compared: Asus, Dell, HP, and Toshiba Side by Side

## Overview

Not all laptop keyboards are created equal. Each manufacturer makes distinct choices about key size, layout, spacing, and feel. We compare five of the most iconic laptop keyboard layouts available to explore in the Keyboard Simulator — helping you understand the differences before you buy, or simply appreciate the variety that exists. Why Laptop Keyboards Differ Laptop keyboards vary because each manufacturer balances different priorities: thinness, key travel, key size, layout efficiency, and aesthetic design. A business laptop like the Dell Latitude prioritizes durability and familiar key placement for office workers. A consumer laptop like the Asus ZenBook balances compactness with usability. Understanding these differences helps you choose the right machine — or simply appreciate the craft behind keyboard design. Asus UX370UAR The Asus UX370UAR keyboard is part of the ZenBook Flip line — a convertible ultrabook designed to be as thin and light as possible. This keyboard features: Chiclet-style keys with backlit illumination. Slightly shallower key travel than traditional laptops (~1.0–1.2mm). Compact layout that omits the numpad for a slimmer profile. Integrated touchpad that blends seamlessly with the palm rest. Dell Latitude 5300 & E7270 The Dell Latitude series is designed for business users and IT professionals who demand a consistent, reliable typing experience. Key characteristics: Tactile feel: Slightly deeper key travel (~1.5mm). Productivity: Dedicated Home/End/PgUp/PgDn keys for workflows. Design: Chiclet layout with spill-resistant key design. Backlighting: Optional backlight for low-light environments. Note: The Dell Latitude E7270 is a slightly older, ultra-portable model with a very similar layout, making it popular in enterprise environments where standardized configurations matter. HP EliteBook 820 G4 HP's EliteBook series is known for premium build quality and military-grade durability. The 820 G4 keyboard features: Full-size keys with a comfortable isolation-style layout. Protection: Spill-resistant keyboard with drain channels. Navigation: Integrated pointing stick (TrackPoint equivalent) for mouse control without lifting hands. Visibility: Backlit option and bright key legends. Toshiba Portege X30-E The Toshiba Portege X30-E is a premium ultrabook with a keyboard designed for mobile professionals.
